/ɡʊdz/

### Example Sentences
1. *"The cafeteria sells all kinds of **goods** — from fresh fruit to hot meals."* — 食堂出售各种**商品**——从新鲜水果到热餐。

  - × *"This **goods** is cheap."* — goods 是复数，要用 are。

2. *"That sandwich looks **gross** — I wouldn't eat it."* — 那个三明治看起来真**恶心**——我可不会吃。
   ![[audio/gross_ex2.mp3]]

### Dialogue
**A:** Eww, what's that smell?  
**B:** I know, it's **gross**. Someone left their lunch in the fridge for a week.

### Grammar & Usage Note
- **gross** 有两个截然不同的含义：① 总的（gross income = 总收入）；② 恶心的。
  - √ *"My **gross** salary is 5000 yuan per month."* — 我的**总**工资是每月5000元。
  - √ *"Don't be **gross** at the dinner table!"* — 别在餐桌上那么**恶心**！
  - × *"This food is **grossly**."* — 应说 "This food is **gross**."（形容词）

### Quiz
- **Fill in the blank**: "The smell from the old fridge was absolutely ______."
  - Answer: **gross**

## 20. group

<font color=purple>┃</font> 组，团体；分组 n., v.

/ɡruːp/
![[audio/group.mp3]]

### Example Sentences
1. *"Our study **group** meets every Tuesday evening in the library."* — 我们的学习**小组**每周二晚上在图书馆见面。
   ![[audio/group_ex1.mp3]]
2. *"The teacher **group**ed us into teams of four for the project."* — 老师把我们**分成**四人一组来做这个项目。
   ![[audio/group_ex2.mp3]]

### Dialogue
**A:** Which **group** are you in for the presentation?  
**B:** I'm in **Group** 3. We're presenting on Friday.

### Grammar & Usage Note
- **group** 作名词：集合名词，强调整体时用单数动词（"The **group** is working well"），强调个体时用复数动词（"The **group** are all talented"）。
  - √ *"Our **group** **is** the best."* — 正确（强调整体）。
  - √ *"The **group** **are** discussing different ideas."* — 正确（强调个体成员）。
  - × *"There is three **group**s."* — 应为 "There **are** three **groups**."

### Quiz
- **Fill in the blank**: "We formed a study ______ to prepare for the final exams."
  - Answer: **group**

## Scenario Dialogue

> **A:** Hey, let me **greet** you properly — how was your morning?  
> **B:** It was **great**! But I'm starving. Let's get lunch. The cafeteria has a **grand** selection today.  
> **A:** I can see that. Look at the **graph** on the wall — it shows how many students eat here.  
> **B:** Interesting. Oh, these **grapes** look fresh! And this whole-**grain** bread is a healthy choice.  
> **A:** I'm trying to eat more **green** vegetables. And the **goods** here are better than last semester.  
> **B:** True. But I still **groan** every time I think about the price.  
> **A:** Don't **grind** your teeth over it. Did you check your **grade** on the group project?  
> **B:** Not yet. I **grasp** the material but I'm not sure about my presentation skills.  
> **A:** Don't be so **grave** about it! And don't look so **green** — you'll do fine!  
> **B:** I guess so. Hey, look out the window — there's a **goose** on the **grass**!  
> **A:** That's not **gross**, that's hilarious! The **group** of students over there are trying to chase it away.  
> **B:** Ha! Look at it move with such **grace**. That **goose** has more **grace** than my dancing!  
> **A:** Well, at least we're not in **grief** over bad grades today. Let's enjoy our lunch!
